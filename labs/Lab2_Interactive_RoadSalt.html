🧮 The Math Behind It
ΔT = i × kf × m
ΔT = Temperature Depression  |  i = van't Hoff Factor  |  kf = 1.86°C/m  |  m = Molality

Selected Salt: NaCl

van't Hoff Factor (i): 2 particles

Concentration (m): 1.0 mol/kg

ΔT = 2 × 1.86 × 1.0 = 3.72°C

New Freezing Point: 0°C - 3.72°C = -3.72°C

🏥 Healthcare Connection

IV saline (0.9% NaCl) has a freezing point of -0.52°C — nearly identical to blood plasma (-0.56°C). The same chemistry that keeps roads safe keeps IV fluids isotonic!
