## 🎯 Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | How to Fix |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Wet ice | Makes ΔH_f too LOW | **Pat dry with paper towels!** |
| Not stirring | Inaccurate temperature | Stir continuously |
| Recording temp while ice remains | Temperature stuck at 0°C | Wait until ALL ice melts |
| Adding too much ice | Can't melt completely | Add slowly, stop at ~20°C drop |
| Not saving work | Lose all data if browser closes | Click save button often! |

## 🧮 Quick Reference Formulas

### For Heating/Cooling (no phase change):
```
Q = m × c × ΔT

Where:
- Q = heat energy (J)
- m = mass (g)
- c = specific heat capacity (J/g·°C)
- ΔT = temperature change (°C)
```

### For Phase Change (melting):
```
Q = m × ΔH_f

Where:
- Q = heat energy (J)
- m = mass of ice (g)
- ΔH_f = heat of fusion (J/g) ← This is what we're finding!
```

### For This Experiment:
```
ΔH_f = −(m_water × c_water × ΔT) / m_ice

Where:
- m_water = mass of warm water (g)
- c_water = 4.184 J/(g·°C)
- ΔT = T_final − T_initial (°C)  ← this is NEGATIVE since water cools
- m_ice = mass of ice (g)
- The negative sign ensures ΔH_f is positive (endothermic process)
```

### Constants to Remember:
- **c_water** = 4.184 J/(g·°C)
- **Theoretical ΔH_f** = 333.55 J/g
- Your experimental value should be close to this!

## ❓ Quick Troubleshooting

**Q: My ΔH_f is way too high (>400 J/g)**
- **A:** You probably had heat loss to surroundings OR ice was colder than 0°C

**Q: My ΔH_f is way too low (<300 J/g)**
- **A:** You probably had wet ice OR didn't wait for all ice to melt

**Q: My two trials are very different**
- **A:** One trial had an error. Review your procedure and identify what went wrong.

**Q: Calculations aren't showing up**
- **A:** Click the "Calculate All Values" button

**Q: Can't generate PDF**
- **A:** Try using Chrome browser. Make sure to select "Save as PDF" not "Print"

   - What is heat of fusion?
