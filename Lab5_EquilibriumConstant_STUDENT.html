Introduction

In any chemical reaction, the reactants react until some products are formed. Then the products react with each other to reform the reactants. Eventually the rate of product formation equals the rate of reactant reformation, and the system is in a state of dynamic equilibrium.

This experiment studies the reaction:

Fe³⁺(aq) + SCN⁻(aq) ⇌ FeSCN²⁺(aq)
Equation 1

At equilibrium, all concentrations remain constant and are determined by the equilibrium constant expression:

KC = [FeSCN²⁺] / ([Fe³⁺][SCN⁻])
Equation 2

FeSCN²⁺ is a highly colored (blood-red) compound that absorbs visible light. We will determine its concentration at equilibrium by comparing the absorbance of each run with a set of standard solutions of known concentration, measured at 447 nm using a Spec 20 spectrophotometer.

⚠️ Safety Precautions

  • Wear safety goggles and gloves at all times.
  • HNO₃ (nitric acid) is corrosive — avoid skin contact.
  • Fe(NO₃)₃ will stain skin and clothing.
  • If any solution contacts skin, wash immediately with water.
  • Dispose of all solutions in the designated waste container.

Part C: Calculation of KC

💡 Calculation Steps:
  1. [Fe³⁺]init = (2.00 × 10⁻³ M) × (VFe / Vtotal)
  2. [SCN⁻]init = (2.00 × 10⁻³ M) × (VSCN / Vtotal)
  3. [Fe³⁺]eq = [Fe³⁺]init − [FeSCN²⁺]eq
  4. [SCN⁻]eq = [SCN⁻]init − [FeSCN²⁺]eq
  5. KC = [FeSCN²⁺]eq / ([Fe³⁺]eq × [SCN⁻]eq)
