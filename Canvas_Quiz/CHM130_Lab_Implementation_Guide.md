# CHM 130 Laboratory Implementation Guide

## Overview
This document provides guidance for implementing the four new CHM 130 laboratory experiences aligned with the General Education Lab Structure Proposal for Summer 2026.

## Lab Structure (3-Hour In-Person Labs)

Each lab follows this standardized format:

### Time Breakdown
- **Pre-lab review content:** 30 minutes
- **Pre-lab questions:** 10-15 minutes
- **Experiment:** 90 minutes
- **Post-lab questions & cleanup:** 30-45 minutes
- **TOTAL:** 3 hours in-person

### Post-Lab Assignment (2 hours, outside of lab)
- 2-3 minute video assignment requiring students to:
  - Summarize lab experience
  - Discuss practical application to future healthcare career
  - Demonstrate understanding through creative presentation

## Lab 1: Aspirin (ASA) Synthesis

**File:** `CHM130_Lab1_ASA_Synthesis.html`

### Learning Objectives
- Understand organic synthesis and esterification reactions
- Apply concepts of limiting reactants and percent yield
- Practice recrystallization techniques
- Recognize pharmaceutical chemistry importance in healthcare

### Key Materials
- Salicylic acid (2.0 g per student)
- Acetic anhydride (5 mL per student)
- Phosphoric acid (catalyst)
- Recrystallization supplies

### Clinical Connections
- Aspirin as analgesic, antipyretic, anti-inflammatory
- Low-dose aspirin for cardiovascular protection
- Patient education about aspirin therapy

### Safety Considerations
- Acetic anhydride irritant with strong odor
- Hot water bath precautions
- Never consume lab-synthesized chemicals

### Implementation Notes
- Can be done individually or in pairs
- Recrystallization step optional if time is limited
- Strong connection to pharmacology and patient care

## Lab 2: Soap Making (Saponification)

**File:** `CHM130_Lab2_Soap_Making.html`

### Learning Objectives
- Understand saponification reactions
- Explore chemistry of fats, oils, and triglycerides
- Learn about micelle formation
- Recognize importance of hand hygiene in healthcare

### Key Materials
- Vegetable oil or coconut oil (25 mL per student)
- Sodium hydroxide pellets (4 g per student) - **INSTRUCTOR MAY PREPARE IN ADVANCE**
- Ethanol (15 mL per student)
- Saturated salt solution for salting out
- Test tubes for soap testing

### Clinical Connections
- WHO hand hygiene guidelines
- Infection prevention and control
- Soap mechanism against bacteria and viruses (including COVID-19)
- Patient education about proper handwashing

### Safety Considerations
- **CRITICAL:** NaOH is highly corrosive - may want instructor to prepare solution
- Chemical-resistant gloves required
- Hot oil and water bath precautions
- Fresh soap may have high pH - not safe for immediate skin contact

### Implementation Notes
- Very hands-on and engaging
- Students can take soap home (after curing 2-4 weeks)
- Strong emphasis on infection control principles
- Test soap's ability to emulsify oil (visual demonstration)

## Lab 3: Red Cabbage pH Indicator

**File:** `CHM130_Lab3_Cabbage_pH_Indicator.html`

### Learning Objectives
- Understand pH scale and acid-base concepts
- Learn how natural pH indicators work
- Practice measuring and interpreting pH
- Recognize importance of pH in biological systems and healthcare

### Key Materials
- Red cabbage (1/4 head per group)
- Test tubes or clear cups (12-15 per group)
- Acids: vinegar, lemon juice, HCl (dilute)
- Bases: baking soda, ammonia cleaner
- Household items to test (10+ options)
- pH paper for verification

### Clinical Connections
- Blood pH and acid-base balance (acidosis/alkalosis)
- Urine pH in diagnosis
- Wound pH and healing
- Stomach acid and medication absorption
- Antacids and heartburn

### Safety Considerations
- Hot cabbage water can cause burns
- Some test solutions may be irritants
- Proper labeling of all solutions

### Implementation Notes
- Highly visual and colorful - great for photos/videos
- Can be done in groups of 2-3 students
- Students create their own pH standard scale
- Easy to relate to clinical scenarios
- Very cost-effective lab

## Lab 4: Ester Synthesis (Smell Lab)

**File:** `CHM130_Lab4_Ester_Synthesis_Smells.html`

### Learning Objectives
- Understand esterification and organic functional groups
- Learn how esters create scents and flavors
- Practice organic synthesis techniques
- Recognize role of organic chemistry in pharmaceuticals

### Key Materials
Per ester (students typically make 2-3 different esters):
- **Banana (Isoamyl acetate):** Isoamyl alcohol + Acetic acid
- **Wintergreen (Methyl salicylate):** Salicylic acid + Methanol
- **Orange (Octyl acetate):** Octanol + Acetic acid
- **Pineapple (Ethyl butyrate):** Ethanol + Butyric acid
- Concentrated sulfuric acid (catalyst) - 3-5 drops per ester
- Hot water bath (70-80°C)

### Clinical Connections
- Methyl salicylate in topical analgesics (Ben-Gay, Icy Hot)
- Ester vs. amide local anesthetics (procaine vs. lidocaine)
- Prodrug esters in medication formulations
- Aspirin as an ester

### Safety Considerations
- **CRITICAL:** Concentrated H₂SO₄ is extremely corrosive
- Methanol is toxic - avoid skin contact
- Butyric acid smells terrible (like vomit) - use in fume hood
- Proper wafting technique for smelling chemicals
- Some esters are flammable

### Implementation Notes
- **MOST FUN LAB** - students love the smell aspect
- Highly engaging and memorable
- Can rotate stations if supplies limited
- Compare to commercial products (bring in Ben-Gay, banana candy, etc.)
- Strong connection to pharmacology

## Differentiation and Accommodations

### For Students with Chemical Sensitivities
- **Labs 1 & 2:** May need to work in well-ventilated areas or fume hoods
- **Lab 3:** Minimal concerns - mostly natural materials
- **Lab 4:** Most challenging - provide respirator or allow remote participation with video observation

### For Students with Visual Impairments
- All labs have verbal components (smell, texture)
- Partner with sighted student for color observations
- Provide tactile models of molecular structures

### For Online/Remote Students
- Provide high-quality video demonstrations
- Virtual lab simulations
- Modified assignments focusing on data analysis from provided results

## Tips for Success

### Instructor Preparation
1. **Test each lab** before student implementation
2. **Prepare reagents in advance** when possible (especially NaOH solution)
3. **Set up stations** before students arrive
4. **Review safety protocols** at start of each lab
5. **Have MSDS sheets** readily available

### Student Engagement
1. **Emphasize clinical connections** throughout
2. **Encourage photos/videos** during lab (for their video assignments)
3. **Share examples** of previous student work
4. **Connect to current events** (COVID-19 for soap lab, pain management for aspirin)
5. **Invite students to bring household items** to test in Lab 3

### Time Management
- Set timers for each section
- Have backup plans if experiments run long
- Consider having some data "pre-collected" as examples
- Post-lab questions can be completed as homework if needed

## Troubleshooting

### Lab 1 (Aspirin)
**Problem:** Low yield
**Solution:** Ensure complete mixing, adequate heating time, thorough drying

**Problem:** Colored product
**Solution:** Impurities from incomplete recrystallization - emphasize this is normal for first attempt

### Lab 2 (Soap)
**Problem:** Soap doesn't separate
**Solution:** May need more salt solution or longer settling time

**Problem:** Very high pH
**Solution:** Expected - explain curing process, emphasize not using on skin immediately

### Lab 3 (Cabbage pH)
**Problem:** Weak color
**Solution:** Boil cabbage longer, use more cabbage, or use distilled water

**Problem:** Colors don't match expected
**Solution:** Natural variation - emphasize relative comparisons rather than exact matches

### Lab 4 (Esters)
**Problem:** Weak or wrong smell
**Solution:** Check heating time, H₂SO₄ amount, and reagent quality

**Problem:** No smell
**Solution:** May have overheated (decomposition) or under-heated (incomplete reaction)
