Post-Lab Questions — Answer Key

Question 1: Basic Calorimetry

Heat required to raise 500 g water from 25°C to 85°C?

Given: m = 500 g, c = 4.18 J/(g·°C), ΔT = 85 − 25 = 60°C

Q = m × c × ΔT
Q = 500 × 4.18 × 60
Q = 125,400 J = 125.4 kJ

Question 2: Unknown Metal

20 g metal at 25°C in 50 g water at 90°C → Tf = 80°C. Find cmetal.

Heat lost by water:
Qwater = 50.0 × 4.184 × (80 − 90) = 50 × 4.184 × (−10) = −2,092 J

Heat gained by metal = +2,092 J

Q = m × c × ΔT
2,092 = 20 × cmetal × (80 − 25)
2,092 = 20 × cmetal × 55
2,092 = 1,100 × cmetal
cmetal = 2,092 / 1,100 = 1.90 J/(g·°C)

Note: This value is higher than any common metal. The closest is aluminum (0.897). This is a teaching moment about experimental error in real-world data.

Question 4: Frozen Lake

A frozen lake doesn't melt immediately when air goes above 0°C because of water's enormous heat of fusion (333.55 J/g). The lake contains millions of kilograms of ice, each gram requiring 333.55 J to melt. The air can only transfer heat slowly. The total energy needed is astronomical — a 10 cm thick ice layer on a 1 hectare pond requires ~3 × 1011 J. That's why spring thaw takes weeks, not hours.

Question 5: Arctic Ice Calculation

Q = m × ΔHf
Q = 2.6 × 1019 g × 333.55 J/g
Q = 8.67 × 1021 J (8.67 zettajoules — about 15 years of total global energy consumption)
