Post-Lab Questions — Answers

1. For an endothermic reaction, if temperature increases, what happens to KC?

KC increases. Raising the temperature of an endothermic reaction adds energy (heat) to the reactant side, which shifts equilibrium toward products, increasing the numerator and decreasing the denominator of the KC expression.

2. For an exothermic reaction, if temperature increases, what happens to KC?

KC decreases. Adding heat to an exothermic reaction shifts equilibrium toward reactants (Le Chatelier's principle), decreasing the concentration of products relative to reactants.

3. For an endothermic reaction, if temperature increases, which direction does the reaction shift?

The reaction shifts toward products. Heat acts as a "reactant" in endothermic reactions, so adding heat drives the reaction forward.

4. For an exothermic reaction, if temperature increases, which direction does the reaction shift?

The reaction shifts toward reactants. Heat is a "product" in exothermic reactions, so adding heat pushes the equilibrium backward to consume the excess energy.
