Sample Calculation (Heavy NaCl):

m = 0.128 mol ÷ 0.050 kg = 2.57 m
ΔT = 2 × 1.86 × 2.57 = 9.56°C

Analysis Questions — MODEL ANSWERS

1. Which solution achieved the lowest temperature?

Heavy NaCl should reach the lowest temperature (~−10°C) because it has the highest molality. This matches prediction since ΔT ∝ molality.

2. Cost-effectiveness calculation:

NaCl: 7.5g × ($60/ton) = $0.000496 for 9.56°C → $0.000052 per °C
CaCl₂: 5.0g × ($300/ton) = $0.00165 for 5.03°C → $0.000328 per °C
NaCl is ~6× more cost-effective (but only works above −10°C)

3. Why switch to CaCl₂ below −10°C?

NaCl's practical effectiveness ends around −10°C (eutectic limitations). CaCl₂ works down to −29°C because it produces 3 ions per formula unit and has a lower eutectic point.

4. Environmental concerns:

(1) Chloride accumulation in waterways—harms freshwater organisms
(2) Groundwater contamination—affects drinking water
(3) Soil damage—kills roadside vegetation
(4) Vehicle/infrastructure corrosion

5. Storm at −5°C—which salt?

NaCl is sufficient. At −5°C, even light NaCl (FP = −3.18°C) may not fully protect, but heavy NaCl (FP = −9.56°C) provides ample margin. CaCl₂ works but costs 6× more—save it for colder temps.
