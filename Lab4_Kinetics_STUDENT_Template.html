Discussion Questions

Q1. Explain why increasing [Br₂] does not increase the rate of this reaction.

Q2. What is the role of H⁺ in this reaction? Why does it appear in the rate law if it is a catalyst?

Q3. The reaction is conducted with a large excess of acetone and HCl relative to Br₂. Why is this necessary for the experimental design to work?

Q4. How did you use R² values to determine the reaction order with respect to Br₂? Which graph gave the best fit and what does that indicate?

Q5. Propose a two-step mechanism for this reaction that is consistent with your experimental rate law.

Your mechanism should identify which step is rate-determining and show why Br₂ is zero order.

Q6. How would you expect the rate constant k to change if you repeated this experiment at a temperature 10°C higher? Explain using the Arrhenius equation concept.
