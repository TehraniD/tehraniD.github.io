The trap most students miss: Distractors aren't "wrong answers." They're written by psychometricians to distract you from the correct answer. They contain accurate nursing information. They sound clinically reasonable. An option being correct-sounding does not make it the correct answer to the question being asked.

02The 5 Power Techniques

Distilled from the 12 techniques in Nugent & Vitale Chapter 7 — the 5 highest-leverage moves you can make on any NCLEX question.

1
Find the Strategic Word
The question hides a word that tells you what to look for. Miss the word, miss the question.

Every NCLEX question contains a strategic word that flips your search. Two categories to spot:

2
The Power of Elimination
You don't need to find the right answer. You need to kill the wrong ones first.

Every distractor you eliminate dramatically increases your odds of being right. Always hunt for what to delete before you choose.

The reframe: Don't ask "which one is right?" Ask "which two can I prove are wrong?" The right answer often reveals itself by elimination, even when you don't know the content cold.
3
The Specific Determiner Trap
Extreme words inside an option are almost always WRONG. Click each to "trash" it.

Nursing — and life — is rarely absolute. When you see these words inside an answer choice, eliminate on sight:

ALWAYS
NEVER
ALL
NONE
ONLY
MUST
EVERY
NOTHING

👆 Tap each to practice the elimination motion. Make it a reflex.

Worked example:
"Which statement about pain is true?"
❌ Pain is always subjective · ✅ Pain ratings should be documented routinely · ❌ Patients never lie about pain · ❌ Vital signs must be elevated with severe pain
You eliminated 3 distractors without knowing any pain content.
4
Spot the 4 Trap Patterns
Tap each card to see how to use it. These eliminate distractors without needing the content.

Even when you don't know the answer, these structural clues let you eliminate distractors:

⚔️
Two Opposites
When two options directly contradict each other.
Strategy: The answer is usually ONE of the two opposites. Eliminate the other two options first, then choose between the opposites using your knowledge.
tap →
👯
Equally Plausible
When two options say essentially the same thing.
Strategy: If they're both right, they're both wrong (only one option can be the answer). Eliminate both immediately.
tap →
☂️
The Global Option
One option that umbrellas the others.
Strategy: The umbrella option is usually the answer. "Assess the client" beats "check vital signs," "review labs," "ask about pain" — because it includes all of them.
tap →
🚫
Denies Client Feelings
Options that dismiss, minimize, or rush past emotions.
Strategy: Eliminate on sight. Anything like "Don't worry," "You'll be fine," "Many people feel that way" — gone. Therapeutic responses acknowledge and invite.
tap →
5
The Priority Hierarchy
When you see FIRST / INITIAL / BEST / MOST IMPORTANT — apply these frameworks IN THIS ORDER.

Work from the bottom of the pyramid up. The first framework that produces a clear winner is your answer.

1 🫁 ABCs — Airway → Breathing → Circulation
Airway issue ALWAYS wins, even over bleeding
2 🛏️ Maslow — Physiologic before Safety before Psychosocial
Physical need beats emotional need
3 📋 Nursing Process — Assess BEFORE Act
When in doubt, the answer is "assess"
4 ⏱️ Acute over Chronic
New problem beats managed problem
5 📉 Unstable over Stable
Deteriorating patient wins
AMNES
Always Make Nurses Evaluate Survival
ABCs → Maslow → Nursing process → Easier waits → Survival first

🎯 The 5 Things You Must Know

  1. The distractor was designed to fool you. Slow down. The "right-looking" option is usually the trap.
  2. If 2+ options look right, reread the stem. You missed a strategic word.
  3. Eliminate first, choose second. Every distractor you kill doubles your odds.
  4. Kill the extremes. "Always / never / all / only" almost always go in the trash.
  5. Priority order: ABC → Maslow → Assessment → Acute over chronic. Every priority question.
