Define Chemical Kinetics & Real-World Examples

8 pts

a) Definition

Full Credit Answer

Chemical kinetics is the study of how fast chemical reactions occur and what factors control that speed (concentration, temperature, catalysts, surface area).

It does not tell you whether a reaction will happen — that is thermodynamics (ΔG). It tells you how quickly the reaction proceeds once it starts.

Grading: 2 pts for “rate/speed of reactions”; 2 pts for listing at least one controlling factor; 2 pts for distinguishing kinetics from thermodynamics.

b) Two real-world examples

Example 1

Food spoilage / refrigeration. Refrigeration slows the bacterial decomposition reaction. Lower temperature means fewer molecular collisions per second, so the food takes much longer to spoil. Rate matters because it determines shelf life.

Example 2

Drug delivery in medicine. Time-release medications are designed so the active ingredient is released at a controlled rate. Too fast = toxic overdose. Too slow = ineffective treatment. Kinetics lets pharmacists engineer the right release rate.

Other acceptable examples: combustion, rust, ozone depletion, fermentation, enzyme reactions. 1 pt per example + 1 pt for explaining WHY rate matters.

C3H8(g) + 5O2(g) → 3CO2(g) + 4H2O(g)

Final Answer Rate = −Δ[C3H8]⁄Δt = −1⁄5 Δ[O2]⁄Δt = +1⁄3 Δ[CO2]⁄Δt = +1⁄4 Δ[H2O]⁄Δt
Filled-in Scaffold

Rate = − Δ[C3H8]/Δt = − (1/5) Δ[O2]/Δt = + (1/3) Δ[CO2]/Δt = + (1/4) Δ[H2O]/Δt

Calculate Ea from Arrhenius Slope

8 pts

Given: slope = −15,200 K  •  R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)

1

Write the formula: slope = −Ea / R

2

Rearrange: Ea = −slope × R

3

Substitute: Ea = −(−15,200 K) × (8.314 J/(mol·K))

= 15,200 × 8.314 = 126,373 J/mol

4

Convert J → kJ: 126,373 ÷ 1000 = 126.4 kJ/mol

Final Answer Ea = 126.4 kJ/mol

2 pts for correct rearrangement; 3 pts for correct multiplication; 1 pt for J→kJ conversion; 2 pts for correct final answer with units.

Conceptual Synthesis: Order from Units + Two-Point Arrhenius

8 pts

Given Data

Temperaturek
300 K1.5 × 10−3 s−1
350 K4.2 × 10−2 s−1

(a) Overall Order from Units — 2 pts

The units of k are s−1.

Using k units = M(1−n)·s−1: we need M(1−n) = M0 = 1 (no molarity unit).

1 − n = 0  ⇒  n = 1

Answer First order (overall order = 1), because k has units of s−1

(b) Calculate Ea (Two-Point Arrhenius) — 6 pts

ln(k2k1) = −EaR · (1T2 − 1T1)
1

T1 = 300 K, k1 = 1.5 × 10−3 s−1

T2 = 350 K, k2 = 4.2 × 10−2 s−1

2

k2/k1 = (4.2 × 10−2) / (1.5 × 10−3) = 28.0

ln(28.0) = 3.332

3

1/T2 − 1/T1 = 1/350 − 1/300 = 0.002857 − 0.003333 = −4.762 × 10−4 K−1

4

3.332 = −(Ea / 8.314) × (−4.762 × 10−4)

Ea = (3.332 × 8.314) / (4.762 × 10−4) = 27.71 / 4.762 × 10−4 = 58,178 J/mol

5

Convert: 58,178 ÷ 1000 = 58.2 kJ/mol

Final Answer Ea ≈ 58.2 kJ/mol
