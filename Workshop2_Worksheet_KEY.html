Define chemical kinetics and give two real-world examples. (8 pts)
Chemical kinetics is the study of the rate (speed) of chemical reactions — how quickly reactants are consumed and products are formed. (4 pts for definition)

Examples (2 pts each, accept any reasonable answers):
• The lifetime of pollutants in the atmosphere (e.g., how quickly ozone decomposes)
• Drug delivery and metabolism in the body
• Food spoilage / effectiveness of preservatives
• Catalytic converters cleaning exhaust gases

Accept any reasonable real-world examples involving reaction speed.

Rate expression for C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O (8 pts)
Rate = −Δ[C₃H₈]/Δt = −(1/5)Δ[O₂]/Δt = +(1/3)Δ[CO₂]/Δt = +(1/4)Δ[H₂O]/Δt

2 pts per correct term. Deduct 1 pt for missing negative signs on reactants or missing 1/coefficient.

Rate law for NO₂ + O₃ → NO₃ + O₂ (14 pts)
Exp[NO₂] M[O₃] MRate (M·s⁻¹)

32.5×10⁻⁵2.0×10⁻⁵0.022
(a) Order in NO₂ (3 pts):
Compare Exp 2 & 3 ([O₃] constant):
Rate₃/Rate₂ = 0.022/0.044 = 0.5
[NO₂]₃/[NO₂]₂ = (2.5×10⁻⁵)/(5.0×10⁻⁵) = 0.5
0.5 = 0.5a → a = 1 (first order in NO₂)
(b) Order in O₃ (3 pts):
Compare Exp 1 & 2 ([NO₂] constant):
Rate₂/Rate₁ = 0.044/0.022 = 2
[O₃]₂/[O₃]₁ = (2.0×10⁻⁵)/(1.0×10⁻⁵) = 2
2 = 2b → b = 1 (first order in O₃)
(c) Rate law (4 pts):
Rate = k[NO₂][O₃] — first order in each, second order overall
(d) Value of k (4 pts):
k = Rate / ([NO₂][O₃]) = 0.022 / (5.0×10⁻⁵ × 1.0×10⁻⁵)
k = 0.022 / (5.0×10⁻¹⁰) = 4.4 × 10⁷ M⁻¹s⁻¹

8
Arrhenius equation and linearized form. (10 pts)
(a) Arrhenius equation (3 pts):
k = A·e−Ea/RT
(b) Variable definitions (4 pts):
• k = rate constant
• A = Arrhenius factor (pre-exponential factor); related to collision frequency and orientation
• Ea = activation energy (J/mol); minimum energy required to initiate reaction
• R = ideal gas constant = 8.314 J/(mol·K)
• T = temperature in Kelvin
(c) Linearized form (3 pts):
ln k = −(Ea/R)·(1/T) + ln A
Plot ln k (y-axis) vs. 1/T (x-axis)
Slope = −Ea/R
y-intercept = ln A
9
Ea from Arrhenius slope of −15,200 K. (8 pts)
slope = −Ea/R
−15,200 K = −Ea / 8.314 J/(mol·K)
Ea = 15,200 × 8.314 = 126,373 J/mol
Ea ≈ 126 kJ/mol

4 pts for setup, 4 pts for correct answer with units. Accept 126–127 kJ/mol.

Conceptual synthesis: two-point Arrhenius problem. (8 pts)
Temperaturek
300 K1.5 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹
350 K4.2 × 10⁻² s⁻¹
(a) Order from units (3 pts):
The units of k are s⁻¹, which corresponds to a first-order reaction.
(For rate = k[A], the units work out: M·s⁻¹ = s⁻¹ × M ✓)
(b) Two-point Arrhenius (5 pts):
ln(k₂/k₁) = −(Ea/R)(1/T₂ − 1/T₁)

ln(4.2×10⁻² / 1.5×10⁻³) = −(Ea/8.314)(1/350 − 1/300)
ln(28) = −(Ea/8.314)(0.002857 − 0.003333)
3.332 = −(Ea/8.314)(−4.762×10⁻⁴)
3.332 = (Ea/8.314)(4.762×10⁻⁴)
Ea = (3.332 × 8.314) / 4.762×10⁻⁴
Ea = 27.71 / 4.762×10⁻⁴
Ea = 58,174 J/mol
Ea ≈ 58.2 kJ/mol
